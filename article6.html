Understanding CANDU Reactors

CANDU (CANada Deuterium Uranium) reactors represent a pinnacle of nuclear engineering innovation. These Canadian-designed heavy water reactors utilize natural uranium fuel and heavy water (deuterium oxide) as both moderator and coolant, creating a highly efficient and safe nuclear power generation system.

Key Components

The CANDU reactor features several sophisticated components including fuel channels, endshields, calandria vessels, and comprehensive safety systems. Each component plays a crucial role in the safe and efficient operation of the reactor.

The Nuclear Process

The process begins with natural uranium converted into fuel pellets, where nuclear fission splits uranium atoms to generate heat. The heavy water moderator slows down neutrons, increasing the probability of fission reactions. This heat is then transferred to steam generators, converting regular water into pressurized steam that drives turbines to generate electricity.

Safety Features

CANDU reactors incorporate multiple layers of safety systems, including passive safety features that operate without external power or human intervention. These systems ensure safe operation under all conditions.
