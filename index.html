Candu Calandria Reactors

CANDU Reactors are Canadian designed heavy water reactors. CANDU Reactors are fueled by natural uranium and utilize heavy water, also known as deuterium as it's moderator and coolant. The natural uranium is converted into fuel pellets and heat is generated by splitting these uranium atoms in a process called nuclear fission. This reaction releases heat into the moderator. The heated heavy water is then moved to steam generators where the heat is transferred to regular water and converted into pressurized steam.The pressurized steam then pushes the turbines blades and causes the rotor to spin. The rotor is a large magnet producing magnetic fields which move across copper wires and produce electricity. CANDU Reactors are comprised of multiple important components such as fuel channels, endshields, and safety systems. This video goes into more detail on CANDU reactor components and their functions.
